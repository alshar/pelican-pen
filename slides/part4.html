There are plenty of things legislators, local governments, and police departments can do to help curb the problems I've laid out

Excerpts from policy recommendations by ACLU Louisiana
Reduce arrests
Enact a statutory presumption of summons in lieu of arrest for most misdemeanors and some felonies.
Limit arrests for non-violent offenses correlated with poverty, homelessness, and chemical dependency.
Bail reform
Repeal Article 315 of the Louisiana Code of Criminal Procedure, which authorizes the use of unconstitutional one-size-fitsall bail schedules.
Eliminate reliance on bail schedules and institute policies for making bail
Racial justice
Require racial impact statements for any legislation related to the criminal legal system.
Excerpts from policy recommendations by the Southern Poverty Law Center
Discourage over-policing
Discourage over-policing by avoiding language that calls for police to patrol “in a proactive manner” or to “aggressively investigate suspected violations of law.”
More transparency
Mandate data collection for all stops – not just those that result in citations – and require publication and periodic review of the data to determine if racial disparities are present in stops, searches, citations, arrests, and uses of force.
Bias training
One example of training that Louisiana law enforcement agencies could introduce comes from California, which requires all officers in the state to be trained on the “[n]egative impact of intentional and implicit biases, prejudices, and stereotyping on effective law enforcement”
